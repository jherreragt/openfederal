Open Federal is the Sunlight Foundation toolbox, an improved way to explore data related to the federal government, coming in late 2015.

These tools and data will power Open Federal:

Lobbying Registration Tracker: The Lobbyist Registration Tracker, updated weekly, shows which special interests have hired new lobbyists within the last 45 days and includes all registrants since 2008.

Foreign Lobbying: Foreign Influence Explorer documents some of the ways foreign entities influence policy and opinions in the United States.The foreign influence data housed here comes from documents collected by the Department of Justice as required by the Foreign Agents Registration Act and press releases from the Defense Security Cooperation Agency.

Real-Time Federal Campaign Finance: The Real-Time Federal Campaign Finance tracker takes official Federal Election Commission data for the current election cycle as it’s filed and presents it in a more user-friendly format.

Capitol Words visualizes the most frequently used words in the Congressional Record, giving you an at-a-glance view of which issues lawmakers address on a daily, weekly, monthly and yearly basis.

Congress API is a clearinghouse of information on legislators, districts, committees, bills, votes, as well as real-time notice of hearings, floor activity and upcoming bills. Learn more about APIs.

Political Party Time which tracks who’s fundraising and where — is a central clearing house of tens of thousands of invitations to fundraisers collected since 2008.

House: The House expenditure reports detail how each House office spends its office budget, including everything from payroll to paper clips. The reports are issued by the Office of the Clerk of the House of Representatives quarterly in PDF files. Sunlight unlocks the data and puts it into an easy-to-use format to give journalists, political scientists and others a clearer picture of how the Senate is spending the taxpayers' money.

Senate: Senate expenditure reports detail how each office spends its office budget, including everything from payroll to paper clips. The reports are issued by the secretary of the Senate twice a year in PDF files. Sunlight unlocks the data and puts it into an easy-to-use format to give journalists, political scientists and others a clearer picture of how the Senate is spending the taxpayers' money.

Open Federal is a product of the Sunlight Foundation, a nonpartisan nonprofit that advocates for open government globally and uses technology to make government more accountable to all.
